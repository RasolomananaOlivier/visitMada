Antananarivo

Fondly known as Tana, Madagascar’s capital is home to Ivato International Airport (TNR) and as such is the first port of call for most overseas visitors. Its central location makes it the ideal base for visiting the rest of the island’s attractions - but it’s also well worth exploring in its own right.

Analakely Market

Tana’s main marketplace is not a tourist attraction, but a bustling, chaotic destination full of local vendors selling everything from traditional fabrics to household items and souvenirs.

Toamasina

The economic capital of Madagascar, the city of Toamasina (Tamatave) offers a wide variety of activities for both young and old. The little ones will appreciate the parks around, the rides in rickshaws and quad, the beach football games, while the big ones will love cultural tours, bars and nightclubs. There are for every taste, the Ivoloina Park for lovers of nature, Fort Manda for fans of cultural visits, headscarf for fans of white sand beach or even Mahambo for surfers.

Fort Manda

Fort Manda was built by King Radama 1st between 1826 and 1831, with England's help to protect Madagascar from the naval attacks coming from the East. His 6 meters of height housed the garrison of the royal army and the residence of the governor merina.

Foulepointe

This coastal resort will offer you postcard-worthy beaches, fine sand and turquoise waters at the rendezvous It will delight more than one with its golf by the sea, its waters conducive to snorkeling or its pirate cemetery. And if you feel the soul of a sailor, many sea trips are at the start of these banks.

Abdallah Awad

Abdallah awad, the Place-to-be. Considered as the best glacier on the big island. You will find different perfume ice and other halal appetizers. They have set up a new space to receive more people.

Calypso Hotel

The Calypso Hotel & Spa is a prestigious 4-star business hotel in a quiet area of downtown Tamatave. Flexibility, professionalism and personalization; their key words to make your stay as comfortable as possible.

Antsiranana

Antsiranana, also known as Diego Suarez, is a port city in northern Madagascar. It sits on Antsiranana Bay, a vast natural harbor made up of several smaller bays. Offshore, conical Nosy Lonja, or Sugar Loaf Island, is considered sacred by locals.

La Bodega

Delicious food. Good atmosphere. Situated in a safe area. The food served is fresh and quite tasty... The service is not slow and the prices are value for money.

Les Trois Baies

Les Trois Baies represent a collection of three warm bays boasting white sand and clear blue water. Sakalava Bay is loved by surfers for its waves and wind, but if you prefer a calmer swim, head to the remaining two beaches. Take snorkeling gear to get to know the marine life, or just stretch out on the sand. The area is generally not crowded on weekdays, so go then if you prefer a solo experience. Refreshments and other services are not offered here, so take plenty of water and snacks. Ankarana Special Reserve

Cross a deep ravine on a suspension foot bridge and admire Jurassic limestone formations at Ankarana Special Reserve, a wildlife reserve with some of the highest population density of primates in the world. Dark karst pinnacles, cave systems, and underground waters with dry deciduous and semi-deciduous forests cover 18,220 hectares (45,023 acres). Watch closely to see crowned lemurs, Sanford's brown lemur, Perrier's black lemur, brown mouse lemur, and dwarf lemurs, and you can also spot chameleons and leaf-tailed geckos. Many birds inhabit the area, including paradise flycatcher and the common jery. Mahajanga

Mahajanga, also known as Majunga, is nicknamed the city of flowers. The port and capital city of the northwest region of Madagascar, it offers a magnificent natural and cultural landscape. Situated at the mouth of the Betsiboka River, many people from all walks of life live together in peace and harmony, offering visitors an unforgettable stay. Many land and sea activities are possible. You won't be bored !

The Baobab

In the middle of the road throne a pluricentenary tree, a baobab of 800 years, a 14 m circumference, which makes the reputation of the city of Mahajanga, northwest of Madagascar.

Antsanitia resort

Located in the northwest of the island, Antsanitia Resort Hotel Madagascar is nested in a tropical garden at the edge of the Indian Ocean. Certified Green Globe ® in 2013, the eco-resort lives in harmony with the fishing hamlets around it. Go back to the basics: the sun, the ocean, a long white sandy beach in Madagascar or a walk in the cool shade of the trees will revive your body and your soul.

Grand Pavois

Grand Pavois is one of the best beaches in Madagascar. White sand, turquoise seas awaits you there. Several restaurants are around, you will not miss it.

Chez Madame Chabaud

The restaurant Chez Madame Chabaud on the island of Katsepy is undoubtedly the address of the brand that has the most charm and exoticism. The restaurant has a park, and it also includes bungalows.
