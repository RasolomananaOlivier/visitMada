Toamasina

The economic capital of Madagascar, the city of Toamasina (Tamatave) offers a wide variety of activities for both young and old. The little ones will appreciate the parks around, the rides in rickshaws and quad, the beach football games, while the big ones will love cultural tours, bars and nightclubs. There are for every taste, the Ivoloina Park for lovers of nature, Fort Manda for fans of cultural visits, headscarf for fans of white sand beach or even Mahambo for surfers.

Best places

Places to see, ways to wander, and signature experiences.

Fort Manda

Fort Manda was built by King Radama 1st between 1826 and 1831, with England's help to protect Madagascar from the naval attacks coming from the East. His 6 meters of height housed the garrison of the royal army and the residence of the governor merina.

Ile aux Prunes Light

This small island is located 17 km from the port of Toamasina. It is surrounded by coral reefs. Day trip, the visit of the lighthouse, the highest in Africa (66 meters), built in 1931, you will discover a breathtaking 360° view, on the Indian Ocean. Snorkeling, the seabed around the island are a real giant aquarium.

Foulepointe

This coastal resort will offer you postcard-worthy beaches, fine sand and turquoise waters at the rendezvous It will delight more than one with its golf by the sea, its waters conducive to snorkeling or its pirate cemetery. And if you feel the soul of a sailor, many sea trips are at the start of these banks.

City Hall

The Tamatave City Hall has a very important role in cultural tourism, thanks to its beauty that marks the specificity and identity of this locality compared to other provinces. The city hall and the Tamatave Independence Avenue are among the factors of attractiveness of this place. Professional guides of Tamatave are ready to satisfy you to discover the fascinating stories of every material heritage around the avenue of independence.
